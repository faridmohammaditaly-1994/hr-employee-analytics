# 👥 Insights — HR Employee Analytics

## 1. Workforce Overview
- Total workforce: 1,470 employees across 3 departments and 9 job roles
- Overall attrition rate: ~16% — above the industry benchmark of 10-12%
- Average tenure: ~7 years — relatively stable workforce
- Average monthly income: ~$6,500

## 2. Attrition by Department and Role
- Sales department has the highest attrition rate
- Laboratory Technician and Sales Representative roles
  show disproportionately high attrition (>20%)
- Research & Development has the lowest attrition
  despite being the largest department

## 3. Compensation Analysis
- Female employees earn more than male employees
  across all three departments
- The pay gap in favor of females is largest in Human Resources
  (~$893/month) and Research & Development (~$384/month)
- Sales shows the smallest gap (~$22/month) — nearly equal pay
- Job level strongly predicts income — level 5 earns
  roughly 3-4x more than level 1

## 4. Tenure and Promotion
- Employees in the 0-2 year bucket have the highest attrition
  — early tenure is the most vulnerable period
- Promotion rate slows significantly after 6 years
  — long-tenured employees feel career stagnation
- Average years since last promotion: ~2 years across all departments

## 5. Satisfaction and Attrition
- Clear negative correlation between job satisfaction and attrition
  — Low satisfaction group has 2x the attrition of Very High group
- Overtime rate does not directly predict attrition —
  Very High satisfaction employees work the most overtime
  yet have the lowest attrition
- Work-life balance scores are consistent across satisfaction levels
  — not a differentiating factor in this dataset
- Key finding: job satisfaction is the strongest predictor
  of attrition, not workload or work-life balance

## 6. Income Progression
- Largest income jump occurs between levels 1 and 2
  — strongest promotion incentive at entry level
- Income growth slows at higher levels
  — senior employees face plateau risk
- Wide income ranges within each level suggest
  inconsistent compensation practices

## 7. Attrition Risk Score
- High Risk employees (119 employees, 8% of workforce) show
  attrition rate of 30.25% — 3x higher than Low Risk group (9.53%)
- Risk score successfully differentiates attrition behavior —
  validating the scoring model
- High Risk employees paradoxically earn the most ($7,699/month)
  — compensation alone does not prevent attrition
- Job satisfaction drops sharply from Low Risk (3.24) to
  High Risk (1.55) — confirming satisfaction as the
  strongest attrition predictor
- High Risk employees have the longest tenure (9.39 years) —
  long-tenured employees are not immune to attrition risk
- Risk weights were updated to reflect actual data:
  job satisfaction carries the highest weight (+3)
  as the strongest proven predictor of attrition
- HR should immediately prioritize the 119 High Risk employees
  for retention conversations — focusing on satisfaction
  and promotion opportunities
